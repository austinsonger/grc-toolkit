# UK GDPR Overview

**Regulation**: UK General Data Protection Regulation
**Authority**: Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
**Jurisdiction**: United Kingdom
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2021 (post-Brexit)
**Applicable To**: Organizations processing personal data of UK residents

---

## Overview

UK GDPR is the UK's post-Brexit version of EU GDPR, retained in UK law via the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018. It operates alongside the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018).

## Key Differences from EU GDPR

| Aspect | EU GDPR | UK GDPR |
|---|---|---|
| Supervisory Authority | Lead DPA in EU | ICO |
| Transfers | SCCs, adequacy decisions | IDTA, addendum to SCCs |
| DPO Registration | No registration required | ICO has register |
| International Transfers | EU adequacy list | UK adequacy list |

## Security Requirements (Article 32)

Same core requirements as EU GDPR:
- Pseudonymization and encryption
- Ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability
- Ability to restore availability after incidents
- Regular testing of security measures

## ICO Guidance on Security

The ICO recommends using recognized standards such as:
- **Cyber Essentials** - UK government-backed certification
- **ISO 27001** - International standard for ISMS
- **NCSC guidance** - National Cyber Security Centre guidelines

## Breach Notification

- Report to ICO: **72 hours** (same as EU GDPR)
- Notify data subjects: Without undue delay when high risk
