# NIS2 Directive

**Directive**: Network and Information Security Directive 2 (NIS2)
**Authority**: European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA)
**Directive Number**: (EU) 2022/2555
**Jurisdiction**: European Union
**Transposition Deadline**: October 17, 2024
**Applicable To**: Essential and important entities in specified sectors

---

## Overview

NIS2 significantly expands the scope and requirements of the original NIS Directive, introducing stricter cybersecurity requirements and broader applicability across sectors.

## Covered Sectors

### Highly Critical (Annex I)
- Energy (electricity, gas, oil, hydrogen)
- Transport (air, rail, water, road)
- Banking and financial market infrastructure
- Health (hospitals, clinical labs, research)
- Drinking water
- Wastewater
- Digital infrastructure (IXPs, DNS, TLDs, cloud, CDN, TSPs)
- ICT service management (B2B MSPs)
- Public administration
- Space

### Critical (Annex II)
- Postal and courier services
- Waste management
- Chemical manufacture and distribution
- Food production and distribution
- Medical device manufacturing
- Digital providers (online marketplaces, search engines, social networks)

## Article 21 – Security Measures

Organizations must implement measures addressing:

| Category | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Policies | Risk analysis and information system security policies |
| Incident Handling | Incident handling, detection, and notification |
| Business Continuity | Backup management, DR, crisis management |
| Supply Chain | Security in supply chain and vendor relationships |
| Procurement | Security in network and information systems acquisition |
| Effectiveness | Policies for assessing security measure effectiveness |
| Training | Cybersecurity hygiene practices and training |
| Cryptography | Policies on use of cryptography and encryption |
| Access Control | Human resources security, access control, asset management |
| Authentication | Multi-factor authentication and continuous authentication |
| Communications | Secured emergency communications |

## Incident Reporting Timeline

| Step | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Early Warning | Within 24 hours of becoming aware |
| Incident Notification | Within 72 hours |
| Intermediate Report | On request of competent authority |
| Final Report | Within 1 month of incident notification |
